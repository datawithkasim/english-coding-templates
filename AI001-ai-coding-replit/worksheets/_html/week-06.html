🤖 AI001 Week 6 — English Worksheet

Topic: Debugging — Explaining Errors to AI · Course: AI Coding with Replit Agent · Time: about 45 minutes

When your code breaks, telling the AI "it doesn't work" is not enough. A good bug report gives three things: what you tried to do, what you expected, and what actually happened — plus the exact error message, copied word for word.

1 · Predict 🔮

Read each bug report. Before the AI reads it, decide whether the AI has enough information to help — and what is missing.

It doesn't work. Fix it.

Does the AI know what "it" is, or what "work" should look like? What is missing?

When I click the button, the score should go up,
but nothing happens.

This says what you tried and what you expected. What one more thing would make it even better?

When I click the button the score should go up but nothing
happens. The console shows:
  Uncaught TypeError: Cannot read property 'textContent' of null
  at index.js:7

Why does pasting the exact error message help the AI find the bug fast?

2 · Fix the Bug Report 🐛

Each bug report below is missing one of the three parts (what you tried / what you expected / what actually happened) or the error message. Read the situation, then rewrite the report so the AI can help.

Report A — A button is supposed to show a message when clicked, but the page does nothing. The student typed:

The button is broken.

Hint: add what you expected ("a message should appear") and what actually happened ("nothing happens when I click").

Write a clear bug report:

What parts did you add?

Report B — The page shows a red error in the console, but the student only wrote:

There's an error somewhere.

Hint: copy the exact error message and the line number into the report.

Write a clear bug report (you can invent a sample error message):

Why does the exact message matter?

Report C — A box was supposed to stay hidden until clicked, but it shows the whole time — the opposite of what was wanted.

The box is wrong.

Hint: explain the opposite situation clearly: "it should be hidden at first, but it is always showing."

Write a clear bug report:

Why is describing the "opposite" situation helpful?

3 · Debug Something Real 📸

Open one of your earlier projects and find a part that does not work the way you want (or ask the AI to add a feature that breaks something). Write a clear bug report with all three parts plus the error message, and let the AI fix it. Then ask: "What did you change to fix it?"

When you finish, send a photo or video of the before and after, then explain what you did. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

The thing that was broken was …

I expected it to … but instead it …

The error message said …

The AI fixed it by …

In my own words, the bug was …

Next time I hit a bug, I will …

4 · Explain the Fix in Your Own Words 🗒️

The AI told you what it changed. Now write it in your own words — like you are explaining to a friend who has never coded. Do not copy the AI's words exactly.
