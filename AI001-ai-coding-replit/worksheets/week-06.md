# 🤖 AI001 Week 6 — English Worksheet

**Topic:** Debugging — Explaining Errors to AI · **Course:** AI Coding with Replit Agent · **Time:** about 45 minutes

When your code breaks, telling the AI **"it doesn't work"** is not enough. A good bug report gives three things: **what you tried to do**, **what you expected**, and **what actually happened** — plus the exact error message, copied word for word.

---

## 1 · Predict 🔮

Read each bug report. Before the AI reads it, decide whether the AI has enough information to help — and what is missing.

```
It doesn't work. Fix it.
```

**Does the AI know what "it" is, or what "work" should look like? What is missing?**

## 3 · Debug Something Real 📸

Open one of your earlier projects and find a part that does not work the way you want (or ask the AI to add a feature that breaks something). Write a clear bug report with all three parts plus the error message, and let the AI fix it. Then ask: **"What did you change to fix it?"**

When you finish, send a **photo or video** of the before and after, then explain what you did. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

> The thing that was broken was …
>
> I expected it to … but instead it …
>
> The error message said …
>
> The AI fixed it by …
>
> In my own words, the bug was …
>
> Next time I hit a bug, I will …

## 4 · Explain the Fix in Your Own Words 🗒️

The AI told you what it changed. Now write it **in your own words** — like you are explaining to a friend who has never coded. Do not copy the AI's words exactly.
