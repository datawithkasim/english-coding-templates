🎨 RS003 Extension — Design Your Game Art with AI

Topic: Prompting Google Gemini for 32-bit Game Sprites · Course: Pygame Turret · Time: about 45 minutes

This is a bonus mission. Your job this week is not coding — it is being the art director. So far your game uses shapes you drew with code. Now you write careful instructions (a prompt) and Google Gemini draws 32-bit style sprites for your game — 3 enemy ships, 1 player ship, and 1 background. A good prompt is like a good recipe: the clearer you are, the better the picture.

🧠 Words to know: prompt, sprite, 32-bit, transparent background, asset

⚠️ Open Gemini together with a grown-up or your teacher. Always check the picture before you use it.

1 · What Makes a Good Prompt? 🔮

A computer artist cannot read your mind. It only knows what you say. A strong sprite prompt names six things:

Part Question it answers Example
Subject What is it? a tiny alien fighter ship
Style What art look? 32-bit pixel art, retro arcade
View From what angle? top-down view, seen from above
Colours What palette? purple and green, glowing
Background What is behind it? plain transparent background, no scenery
Use Where will it go? a game sprite for a 2D shooter

Read this prompt. Underline the six parts above inside it:

"A tiny alien fighter ship, 32-bit pixel art, retro arcade style, top-down view seen from above, purple and green with glowing lights, on a plain transparent background, as a game sprite for a 2D shooter."

Why do we ask for a transparent background? (Hint: think about putting the ship on top of your own sky.)

2 · The Prompt Recipe 🧩

Here is your fill-in-the-blank recipe. Copy it, swap the blanks for your own ideas, and you have a prompt. The words in bold stay the same every time — they are what make it look like a game sprite.

A ____________ (what it is),
in 32-bit pixel art, retro arcade style,
top-down view seen from above,
____________ colours (your palette),
on a plain transparent background, no scenery,
as a single game sprite for a 2D Pygame shooter.

Fill the recipe for ONE idea right now — your player ship:

A __________

in 32-bit pixel art, retro arcade style, top-down view seen from above,

______ colours,

on a plain transparent background, no scenery, as a single game sprite.

5 · Make It 📸

🎯 Generate and save all five assets

  1. Open Google Gemini with your teacher or a grown-up.
  2. Paste one prompt at a time. If the picture is wrong, change one word and try again — that is real prompt engineering.
  3. Save each finished picture (for example player.png, enemy1.png, enemy2.png, enemy3.png, background.png).
  4. You should end with 5 images: 1 player ship, 3 enemy ships, and 1 background.

💡 No coding this week. Your job is the art only — a later week swaps these pictures into the game.

Record a short video of yourself talking through your art. You are not showing a game — you are explaining your prompts and why you chose the words you did. Try to use these words: prompt, sprite, 32-bit, transparent background, asset.
