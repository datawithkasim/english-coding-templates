## 3 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Now switch to your homework world. Build a **7×7 square floor** using a nested loop (just change your `size` variable to 7). Then stand on the floor and check that you can see the center. When you finish, come back here.

Send a photo or video of what you built, then explain what you did. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

> First, I …
>
> My outer loop repeated … times.
>
> My inner loop repeated … times.
>
> The total number of blocks was … because …
>
> The hardest part was …
>
> A nested loop is useful when …

## 4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Now take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) while you walk the camera around what you built. Talk through it like you are teaching someone who has never seen it. Try to use these words: **nested loop**, **outer**, **inner**, **repeat**, **block**.

> 1. Show what your nested loop built.
> 2. Read your outer loop and inner loop out loud and say what each one does.
> 3. Show one bug you hit and how you fixed it.
> 4. Say why a nested loop is better than writing many loops one after another.
