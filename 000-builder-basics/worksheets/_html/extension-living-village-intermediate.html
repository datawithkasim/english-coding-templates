🏰 Builder Basics — The Living Village (Extension · Intermediate)

Topic: Bring It All Together · Course: Builder Basics · Level: Intermediate · Type: Extension Activity · Time: about 60 minutes

🧩 This is a bonus challenge, not a weekly lesson. Try it after Complete Your Village, once you can build with loops, move the agent between spots, and place water and animals.

1 · The Challenge 🎯

Make your village feel like a place someone could live and work. There is no map — you choose the sizes and places, and it must have all eight:

🌾 A farm — rows of crops 🏠 A farm house 🗼 A house with a watchtower 🧱 A fence — around the farm or the animals 💧 A pond with fish 🌉 A bridge over the pond 🪣 A well 🐄 A few animals

⚠️ One rule: everything you can build is built with code. No placing blocks by hand. (Animals are spawned.)

Why does building eight things with code beat placing them by hand one block at a time?

Pick the two hardest parts to build. What makes each one tricky?

3 · Think in Loops 🔁

A loop is for anything you repeat — a row of crops, the four sides of a fence, the planks of a bridge, the levels of a tower.

Choose one part. Describe the loop: what does it repeat, and how many times?

The pond and the bridge go together. Why is it easier to build the pond first and the bridge second?

Between one part and the next, what must the agent do so builds don't land on top of each other?

4 · Show Your Work 📸🎥

Go to your homework world and build one part at a time, moving the agent between them. Build the water first, then span the bridge across it — keep going until all eight parts are placed by code, plus the animals.

Record one video — one take, no stopping (a phone is fine). Show these in order:

1 · Your code. Scroll through it. Say what each part does.

2 · Your build. Point the camera. Name the parts.
