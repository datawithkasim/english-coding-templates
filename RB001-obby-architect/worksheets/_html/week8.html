🏃 RB001 Week 8 — English Worksheet

Topic: Ship It — Publish & Present · Course: Obby Architect · Time: about 45 minutes

This week you ship your obby. In Roblox Studio: File → Publish to Roblox, give your game a real name and description, set it to Public in Game Settings → Permissions, and add a game icon. Then you present it.

1 · Predict 🔮

This week you read your own code. Open your favorite script in your game — the one you are most proud of.

Copy your favorite script here (or the most important part of it):

What does this script do? Walk through it line by line in your own words.

Predict: if you deleted this script and pressed Play, what would break first in your game?

2 · Spot the Bug 🐛

Each launch checklist below has a problem. Read what the student wanted, find what went wrong, then write the fix and explain it.

Bug A — Mina published her obby and sent the link to her friend. Her friend sees "This experience is unavailable" and cannot play.

Game name:    Mina's Mega Obby
Description:  Jump through 5 worlds of lava and vanishing bridges!
Published:    yes
Public:       OFF (Private)
Game icon:    uploaded

Write the fix:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

Bug B — Junho's game is public, but nobody who finds it knows what it is or wants to click it.

Game name:    Untitled Game
Description:  (empty)
Published:    yes
Public:       ON
Game icon:    default gray icon

Write the fix:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

Bug C — Sora published her game on Monday. On Tuesday she built a whole new lava section in Studio. Her friend plays the game online — but the new section is not there.

Game name:    Sora's Sky Run
Description:  Climb to the clouds. Don't touch the lava!
Published:    yes — on Monday
New section:  built Tuesday, saved in Studio only
Public:       ON

Hint: changes in Studio only go online when you do one thing again.

Write the fix:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

3 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Now publish your obby: File → Publish to Roblox, write a real name and a 1–2 sentence description, turn on Public, and add an icon. Open your game's page on the Roblox website. When you finish, come back here.

Send a photo or video of your game's public page, then tell the story of your game. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

My game is called … and in it you …

The hardest bug I fixed was …

I fixed it by …

My favorite script is … because it …

If I kept building, the next thing I would add is …

4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

This is your presentation video. Take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and present your published game like a real developer. Try to use these words: publish, public, description, bug, script.

  1. Show your game's page on the Roblox website and say its name and what kind of game it is.
  2. Play through one full section of your obby.
  3. Open your favorite script and say what it does.
  4. Tell the hardest bug you fixed in this course and how you fixed it.
  5. Say what you would build next.

Write what you will say in your video. Use the space below to plan it before you record — you can read from it while filming.
