🐉 M003 Week 6 — English Worksheet (Intermediate)

Topic: Recreate the Ender Dragon (x, y, z) · Course: 3D Coordinates · Level: Intermediate · Time: about 40 minutes

A big blocky ender dragon stands in your world — black blocks, purple eyes. A big model is just a set of parts: a long body, a head at the front, and two wings that spread out. Today you build the body, the head, and two simple wings. Every block uses (x, y, z) — x across, y up, z forward (deep). The body is long along z. The wings spread along x.

🔴 Stand on your home spot (red block) every run. Move your feet, move your head.

1 · Predict 🔮

Read each code. Write your full prediction.

fill black from (0, 6, 0) to (0, 6, 8)

One block wide, long along z. Across, up, or deeper? How long?

fill black from (1, 6, 4) to (4, 6, 4)

This is one wing. Which number changes? Does it go left/right (x), up (y), or deep (z)?

fill black from (0, 6, 0) to (0, 6, 8)
place black block at (0, 7, 9)

The body, then the head. Is the head in front of the body or behind it? Higher or same height?

2 · Spot the Bug 🐛

Bug A — This should be a long body running deep (along z), from (0, 6, 0) to (0, 6, 8). Right now it is only one block.

fill black from (0, 6, 0) to (0, 6, 0)

Write the fixed code:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

Bug B — This should be a wing spreading out to the side (along x), from (1, 6, 4) to (4, 6, 4). Right now it grows up instead of out.

fill black from (1, 6, 4) to (1, 9, 4)

Write the fixed code:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

3 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Look at the dragon in your world. Walk around it. Then build your own on your home spot, part by part:

fill black from (0, 6, 0) to (0, 6, 8)
place black block at (0, 7, 9)
fill black from (1, 6, 4) to (4, 6, 4)
fill black from (-4, 6, 4) to (-1, 6, 4)

That is body, head, right wing, left wing. Add two purple eye blocks on the head. Make the wings match — same length on both sides.

Send a photo or video. Then finish these — 4 to 6 sentences.

First, I built the body. It runs deep, so I changed the … number.

My head block is at (x = …, y = …, z = …).

The right wing goes from ( … , … , … ) to ( … , … , … ).

To make the left wing match, I …

One tricky moment was when …

If I had more time, I would …

4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Film yourself showing your dragon. Use these words: x, y, z, body, head, wing, home spot.

  1. Show your home spot. Why stand on it every run?
  2. Point at the body. Say which number made it long.
  3. Point at one wing. Say which number made it spread out.
  4. Show that both wings are the same length.
