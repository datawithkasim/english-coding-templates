🧊 M003 Week 8 — English Worksheet (Beginner)

Topic: Final — My Own Museum · Course: 3D Coordinates · Level: Beginner · Time: about 40 minutes

This is your big final build. No new commands — everything from this course working together. Your museum has a room to walk into, a flat picture on the wall (2D, drawn with x and y), and a solid sculpture on a pedestal (3D, placed with x, y, z).

1 · Predict 🔮

Read the steps. Before you imagine it happening, circle or write your answer.

# a picture on the back wall — z stays 0, blocks climb with x and y
place red block at (1, 1, 0)
place red block at (2, 1, 0)

These blocks stay at z = 0 and only x and y change. Is this a flat picture, like a painting on a wall? Circle one: yes · no

Why?

# a sculpture — all three numbers used
fill stone from (4, 0, 4) to (5, 1, 5)
place gold block at (4, 2, 4)

This uses x, y, and z. Is this a solid thing you can walk all the way around? Circle one: yes · no

2 · Find the Difference 🐛

Each pair shows clean steps first, then a broken version of the same idea. Circle what's different and write one short sentence about the bug.

Pair A — The museum room should be hollow so visitors can walk in.

# clean
fill stone from (0, 0, 0) to (8, 4, 8)
fill air from (1, 1, 1) to (7, 3, 7)

# buggy
fill stone from (0, 0, 0) to (8, 4, 8)

What is wrong? Can a visitor get inside the buggy museum?

Pair B — The statue should rest on top of its pedestal, at height 2.

# clean
fill stone from (4, 0, 4) to (5, 1, 5)
place gold block at (4, 2, 4)

# buggy
fill stone from (4, 0, 4) to (5, 1, 5)
place gold block at (4, 0, 4)

What is wrong?

3 · Plan Your Museum 🗺️

Plan before you build. Looking down from above, write where each piece goes.

My room: from ( … , … , … ) to ( … , … , … )

My wall picture shows … and stays at z = …

My pedestal: from ( … , … , … ) to ( … , … , … ) · my sculpture on top at ( … , … , … )

4 · Build Your Museum 📸

Now switch to your homework world and build your museum from your plan. Check your build against this list:

☐ A room with a floor and walls that you can stand inside.

☐ A doorway to walk in through.

☐ At least one flat picture on a wall (2D — only x and y change).

☐ At least one sculpture on a pedestal (3D — x, y, and z).

Send a photo or video of your museum, then explain what you did. Use these sentence starters — write 2 or 3 sentences.

My museum has …

My flat picture is 2D because …

My sculpture is 3D because …

5 · Record Your Tour 🎥

Take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and give a tour of your museum. Talk like you are showing a visitor around. Try to use these words: museum, 2D, 3D, picture, sculpture.

  1. Walk in through the doorway.
  2. Stop at your flat picture and say why it is 2D.
  3. Walk around your sculpture and say why it is 3D.

Write what you will say in your video. You can read from it while filming.
