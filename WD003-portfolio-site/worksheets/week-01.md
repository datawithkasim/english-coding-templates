# 📝 WD003 Week 1 — English Worksheet

**Topic:** Site Planning — Sketch on Paper First · **Course:** Portfolio Site · **Time:** about 45 minutes

This week there is **no code yet**. You plan your site on paper first — a **wireframe**. A wireframe shows *what goes where*, with no colors and no real text. Just boxes and labels. Planning first means less getting lost later.

> A portfolio site usually has these sections: **Hero**, **About**, **Projects**, **Contact**, and a **Footer**. You will arrange them, then decide what *yours* says about *you*.

## 3 · Plan Your Own Site 📸

Now make *your* wireframe. On paper, sketch your site as a column of boxes — one box per section. Inside each box, write only a **label** and one note about what it shows.

1. Draw at least the five sections: **Hero, About, Projects, Contact, Footer**.
2. Pick **three words** that represent you (for example: *coding, funny, creative*).
3. Choose **2 colors and 1 font** you might use, and write them in the margin.
4. Write **3–4 sentences** of self-introduction for your About section.

When you finish, send a **photo or video** of your paper sketch, then explain your plan. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

> The three words I picked for myself are …
>
> My Hero section will say …
>
> In my About section, I want to tell visitors …
>
> The hardest part of planning was …
>
> I chose these colors because …
>
> If I had more time, I would add …

## 4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Now take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and walk through your paper plan. Talk through it like you are showing someone what your finished site *will* look like. Try to use these words: **wireframe**, **section**, **Hero**, **About**, **layout**.

> 1. Hold up your sketch and point to each section from top to bottom.
> 2. Read your three words and say why they fit you.
> 3. Read your About sentences out loud.
> 4. Say which section you are most excited to build.
