🚀 WD003 Week 8 — English Worksheet

Topic: Final Polish — Publish Your Portfolio · Course: Portfolio Site · Time: about 45 minutes

This is the final week. You bring together every section from the past seven weeks, fix anything awkward, and publish your site so anyone can visit it. This is no longer practice — this is your work, going out into the world.

Publishing means putting your site at a real web address. You can use Replit's Webview share link or GitHub Pages. When you have a link, anyone can open it.

1 · Predict 🔮

Read each situation below. Before you check your own site, write what you expect.

A visitor opens your site on a phone for the first time.

Walk through what they see, from the Hero to the Footer. Does every section still look right on a small screen?

A visitor clicks your dark mode button, then refreshes the page.

If you added the localStorage bonus, what should happen? If you did not, what happens instead?

A visitor fills in your contact form and clicks Send.

Describe, in order, what they should see. If any step is missing on your site, note it here so you can fix it.

2 · Spot the Bug 🐛 (Final Checklist)

Go through your own site and find what is broken or unfinished. For each item, write what is wrong and how you will fix it. A great submission is honest — finding your own bugs is the skill.

Check A — Sections. Open your site and confirm all five are present and complete: Hero, About, Projects, Contact, Footer.

What is missing or unfinished? How will you fix it?

Check B — Design system. Are your colors, font, and spacing consistent across every section? Or does one section look like it came from a different site?

Where is it inconsistent? How will you fix it?

Check C — Features. Does the dark mode toggle work? Does the contact form show a thank-you message? Do the nav links scroll smoothly? Does the page hold up on mobile?

Which feature is broken or rough? How will you fix it?

3 · Publish and Present 📸

Finish, polish, and publish your site.

  1. Fix everything you found in the checklist above.
  2. Make sure you have at least three project cards.
  3. Walk through the whole site top to bottom and tidy the spacing, font sizes, and colors.
  4. Publish with Replit's Webview link or GitHub Pages, and open the public link to confirm it works.
  5. Send the link to a friend or family member and ask what they think.

When you finish, send your public link plus PC and mobile screenshots, then present your site in writing. Use these sentence starters — write 5 to 7 sentences total.

The public link to my site is …

The five sections of my site are …

I set up my design system by …

My dark mode works by …

On mobile, my site changes by …

My favorite part is …

The hardest part of the whole project was …

4 · Record Your Final Presentation 🎥

Now record your final presentation video on your phone (or a parent's phone). This is the big one — you are showing off eight weeks of work. Talk through it like you are presenting your site to an audience. Try to use these words: portfolio, design system, dark mode, responsive, published.

  1. Open your published link and scroll through every section.
  2. Explain what each section shows.
  3. Toggle dark mode and show your site on a narrow screen.
  4. Say what you are most proud of, and what was hardest.

Write what you will say in your video. Use the space below to plan it before you record — you can read from it while filming.
