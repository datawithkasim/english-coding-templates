🌍 WD003 Extension — Design a Carbon Footprint Tracker

Topic: Brand Kit + Wireframe for an Environmental Web App · Course: Portfolio Site · Level: Extension · Time: about 60 minutes

This is an extension mission, and there is no code in it. In week 1 you designed a brand kit and a wireframe for a site about you. This time you design for something bigger than you: the environment. Everything we do has a carbon footprint — the CO₂ released into the air by our travel, our food, and our electricity. Scientists track it because what gets measured can be improved. Your mission: plan a web app called a carbon footprint tracker — a page where someone enters their week and sees how big their footprint is, plus one tip to shrink it.

Designers do this for real. Before any app gets built, someone has to decide what it asks, what it shows, and how it feels. That someone is you today.

3 · Design the Brand Kit 🎨

In week 1 your brand kit was about you. This brand belongs to a cause — so every choice should make a visitor think about the planet, and feel like they can do something, not like they are in trouble.

  1. Name the app. Short, friendly, easy to say. Write two or three ideas, then circle the winner.
  2. Pick three words the app should feel like (for example: hopeful, clean, honest). A guilt-trip app and a hopeful app look very different — your words decide which one you are making.
  3. Choose your colors. One main color and one background color that fit your three words. Think about what colors people already connect with nature — and whether you want to follow that or surprise them.
  4. Choose your font style. No font name needed — rounded or sharp? thick or thin? Write the app's name in that style. That is your logo for now.

Stuck for ideas? Designers share their work on sites like dribbble.com and pinterest.com — try searching "carbon tracker app" or "eco app design" and notice what the good ones have in common. One caution: these sites are the open internet, and anyone can post anything, so you may run into things that are off-topic or not for kids. Stay on your search, and if something looks wrong, close it and move on. You are there to get inspired, not to copy a design box for box.

Write your brand kit below too: app name, three words, colors, font style.

4 · Draw the Wireframe 📐

Now decide what goes where. On paper, sketch the tracker as a column of boxes — one box per section, in the order a visitor meets them. Decide which questions your tracker asks (travel? food? electricity? screen time?) and what the Result and Tips sections show.

The same rules from week 1 apply:

Boxes and labels only. No colors, no real pictures, no finished sentences — that comes later.

Name every box. A box nobody can name is a box nobody needed.

One short note per box saying what it shows — "4 questions about my week", "big number + friendly message".

Neat enough that a stranger could read it. If someone else picked up your paper, could they tell what this app does? That is the test.

When your sketch is done, explain your plan. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

My app is called … because …

The three words I picked for it are …

My tracker asks about … because …

After the visitor sees their number, my app shows them …

The hardest design decision was …

If I had more time, I would add …

5 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and pitch your app like you are presenting it to a team that might build it. Try to use these words: carbon footprint, brand kit, wireframe, section, layout.

  1. Say the app's name and what it does in one sentence.
  2. Hold up your brand kit. Read your three words and say why your colors fit them.
  3. Hold up your wireframe and point through it from top to bottom, like a visitor scrolling.
  4. Say one thing about the planet you hope your app would change.

Write what you will say in your video. Plan it below before you record — you can read from it while filming.
