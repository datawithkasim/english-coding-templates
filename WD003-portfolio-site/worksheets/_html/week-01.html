📝 WD003 Week 1 — English Worksheet

Topic: Brand Kit + Wireframe — Plan Before You Build · Course: Portfolio Site · Time: about 60 minutes

This week there is no code yet. Real designers do two things before they touch a computer: they decide how the site should feel (a brand kit) and they decide what goes where (a wireframe). You will do both, on paper. Planning first means less getting lost later.

A portfolio site usually has these sections: Hero, About, Projects, Contact, and a Footer. You will arrange them, then decide what yours says about you.

3 · Design Your Brand Kit 🎨

A brand kit is a small set of choices that make everything you build look like you made it. Big companies have one. Now you do too. Work through these steps on one sheet of paper — that page becomes your brand kit.

  1. Pick three words that describe you (for example: curious, funny, calm). Every choice after this should match your words.
  2. Choose your colors. Pick one main color and one background color that fit your three words. A calm person and a loud person should not end up with the same colors.
  3. Choose your font style. You do not need a font name yet — just decide: rounded or sharp? thick or thin? Write a small sample of your name in that style.
  4. Write your name the way it will appear at the top of your site. That is your logo for now.

Stuck for ideas? Designers share their work on sites like dribbble.com and pinterest.com — searching "portfolio website" there shows you hundreds of real designs, and noticing what you like trains your eye. One caution: these sites are the open internet, and anyone can post anything, so you may run into things that are off-topic or not for kids. Stay on your search, and if something looks wrong, close it and move on. And remember — you are there to get inspired, not to copy someone's design box for box.

Write your brand kit below too: your three words, your colors, your font style.

4 · Draw Your Wireframe 📐

Now decide what goes where. On paper, sketch your site as a column of boxes — one box per section, in the order a visitor will see them.

A good wireframe follows these rules:

Boxes and labels only. No colors, no real photos, no finished sentences — that all comes later.

Name every box. A box nobody can name is a box nobody needed.

One short note per box saying what it shows — "3 project cards", "photo + intro".

Neat enough that a stranger could read it. If someone else picked up your paper, could they tell what each part of your site is? That is the test.

Sketch your Hero, About, Projects, Contact and Footer, then explain your plan. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

The three words I picked for myself are …

I chose these colors because …

My Hero section will say …

In my About section, I want to tell visitors …

The hardest part of planning was …

If I had more time, I would add …

5 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Now take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and walk through your plan. Talk through it like you are showing someone what your finished site will look like. Try to use these words: brand kit, wireframe, section, Hero, layout.

  1. Hold up your brand kit. Read your three words and say why your colors fit them.
  2. Hold up your wireframe and point to each section from top to bottom.
  3. Say which section you are most excited to build.
