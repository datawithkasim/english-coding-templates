🎮 RB005 Week 7 — English Worksheet

Topic: Polish & Store Page — Ready for Strangers · Course: Dream Game Studio · Time: about 45 minutes

This week your game gets its store page: a title, a description, and an icon screenshot. The big question for everything: could a stranger play your game without your help?

1 · Predict 🔮

Read each store page. Before you imagine a real player seeing it, write what you think they will do.

Title: OBBY!!!! FREE ROBUX!!!!! The game: a coin-collecting maze. There is no free Robux.

A player joins because of this title. What do they expect? What do they do when they find a maze instead?

Title: my game Description: this is my game i made it in class

A stranger reads this page. Do they know what kind of game it is or what to do in it? Will they press Play? Why or why not?

Title: Coin Castle: Escape the Tower! Description: Collect 10 coins to unlock the castle door. Dodge the lava bridge and reach the roof to win!

A stranger reads this page. What do they know about the game before joining? What will they try to do first?

2 · Spot the Bug 🐛

This week the bugs are not in code — they are broken store pages. Read what each one is supposed to do, write your fixed version, then explain why the original was wrong and why your fix works.

Bug A — The title should be short, honest, and exciting. The game is a lava-jumping obby with a boss door at the end.

Broken title: 💯FREE PETS GIVING AWAY ROBUX SIMULATOR💯

Write a fixed title:

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

Bug B — The description should tell a stranger what the game is and what to do. The game is a maze where you collect 8 keys to open the exit.

Broken description: good game pls play

Write a fixed description (2–3 sentences):

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

Bug C — The icon should make the game look fun in one glance. This icon is a blurry screenshot of Roblox Studio, with the Explorer window, the toolbar, and a script editor all visible.

Write what screenshot you would take instead (where in the game, what is in the shot, what is NOT in the shot):

Why was it wrong? Why does your fix work?

3 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Now polish your own game's store page. Do these in order:

Write 2 candidate titles for your game, then circle the one you choose:

Write your description (2–3 sentences — clear, honest, exciting):

Checklist hunt — test your game like a stranger and tick each one:

  • [ ] Spawn point is set — new players appear in the right place
  • [ ] The win works — you can actually finish the game
  • [ ] Every menu button does something
  • [ ] Instructions tell a stranger what to do
  • [ ] A stranger could play it without your help

Send a photo or video of your icon screenshot and finished store page, then explain your choices. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

I chose the title … because …

My description tells players …

For my icon, I took a screenshot of …

During the checklist hunt, I found …

One tricky moment was when …

If I had more time, I would …

4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Now take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) — but this week, make it a 30-second trailer. Narrate your game like an advert that makes a stranger want to play. Try to use these words: title, description, icon, player, goal.

  1. Say your game's title and what kind of game it is.
  2. Show the coolest moment of your game while you talk.
  3. Tell players the goal — what do they do to win?
  4. End with one sentence that makes people want to press Play.

Write what you will say in your video. Use the space below to plan it before you record — you can read from it while filming.
