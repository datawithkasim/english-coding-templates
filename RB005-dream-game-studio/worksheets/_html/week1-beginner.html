🎮 RB005 Week 1 — English Worksheet (Beginner)

Topic: Design Your Game — One Mechanic, One Map · Course: Dream Game Studio · Level: Beginner · Time: about 30 minutes

This is YOUR game now. Big games start small: one mechanic, one map.

1 · Predict 🔮

Read each question. Circle or write your answer.

You have 4 weeks. Can you finish a game with 100 levels, pets, AND multiplayer in 4 weeks? Circle one: yes · no

Why?

A game has one mechanic (collect coins) and one map. Can you finish version 1 in 4 weeks? Circle one: yes · no

2 · Find the Difference 🐛

Each pair shows a good design first, then a broken version of the same idea. Circle what's different and write one short sentence about the problem.

Pair A — A design you can really build in 4 weeks.

# good
Players collect 10 coins to open the
gold door on one castle map.

# too big
Open world, 100 levels, pets, a shop,
multiplayer battles, build your own city.

Which one can you really build? What makes the other one too big?

Pair B — Every game needs a win condition.

# good
You win when you touch the gold flag.

# broken
You win when ... (nothing written)

What is wrong? When does the broken game end?

3 · Fill the Gap ✏️

Version 1 of your game should stay small. One word is missing. Fill it in using the word bank.

My game has ONE ____ and ONE map.
You win when you touch the gold flag.

Word bank: mechanic · pet · world

4 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Now write YOUR design doc on paper: your game in one sentence, how you win, and the ONE feature you will build first. When you finish, come back here.

Send a photo or video of your design doc, then explain it. Use these sentence starters — write 2 or 3 sentences.

My game in one sentence is …

You win when …

For version 1, I picked …

5 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and pitch your game like you are telling a friend. Try to use these words: mechanic, win, map.

  1. Say your game's name and your one-sentence idea.
  2. Say what the player does and how they win.
  3. Say the ONE thing you will build first.

Write what you will say in your video. You can read from it while filming.
