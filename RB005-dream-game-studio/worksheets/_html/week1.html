🎮 RB005 Week 1 — English Worksheet

Topic: Design Your Game — One Mechanic, One Map · Course: Dream Game Studio · Time: about 45 minutes

This is YOUR game now. Before you build anything, you need a plan: what does the player do, and how do they win? Great games start small — one mechanic, one map.

1 · Think 🔮

Read each question. Write what you think before class.

A core loop is what the player does again and again. In tag, the loop is: run → chase → tag → run again. What is the loop in an obby (jumping game)?

A win condition tells the player when they are done. Imagine a game with no win condition — it just never ends. Why might players quit a game that never says "You win!"?

You have 4 weeks. Game A has one mechanic and one map. Game B has pets, 100 levels, and an open world. Which one will actually be FINISHED in 4 weeks? Why?

2 · Fix the Design 🐛

A student wrote this design. It sounds amazing — and it is way too big to ever finish.

My game: An open world with 100 levels, a pet system, a shop, multiplayer battles, and a city you can build yourself.

Your job: cut it down to one mechanic, one map.

Which ONE part would you keep for version 1? Why that one?

Rewrite the design as one sentence with one mechanic and one map. (Shape: "Players ___ to ___ on one ___ map.")

The cut features are not deleted forever — they go on a "later list." Why is a later list better than trying to build everything now?

3 · Tell Me What You Built 📸

Now write YOUR design doc — on paper or in a doc. It needs four things: your game in one sentence, the loop (what the player does every 30 seconds), the win condition, and 3 dream features with a circle around the ONE you will build for version 1.

Send a photo or video of your design doc, then explain it here. Use these sentence starters — write 4 to 6 sentences total.

My game in one sentence is …

Every 30 seconds, the player …

You win when …

My three dream features are …

For version 1, I circled … because …

If version 1 goes well, next I would add …

4 · Record Your Walkthrough 🎥

Now take a video on your phone (or a parent's phone) and pitch your game in 1 minute, like you are selling it to a friend. Try to use these words: mechanic, loop, win condition, version 1, player.

  1. Say your game's name and your one-sentence idea.
  2. Explain the loop: what the player does every 30 seconds.
  3. Say the win condition.
  4. Say which ONE feature you picked for version 1 — and why you cut the rest.

Write what you will say in your video. Use the space below to plan it before you record — you can read from it while filming.
